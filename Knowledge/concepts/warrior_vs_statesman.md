# Warrior vs. Statesman

## Definition

A signature framework describing two leadership modes for Scrum Masters:

- **The Warrior:** Direct confrontation, forcing change by sheer will. Acts on instinct. Fills silence. Pushes through obstacles. Effective short-term but unsustainable.
- **The Statesman:** System-level thinking, patience, creating conditions for change. Holds space. Builds coalitions. Sustainable but requires confidence to wait.

Kaan's brand arc is explicitly **Warrior → Statesman** — his early career was Warrior-mode ("The Warrior's Mistake"), and his current philosophy is Statesman-mode with Warrior reserves.

## Current Rule

- The Warrior/Statesman framework is a **brand-voice amplifier**, not a pillar. It can be applied to any pillar.
- Use sparingly — overuse dilutes the metaphor. Best deployed when the post has a genuine tension between the two modes.
- Always ground in a specific moment when the author chose one mode over the other.
