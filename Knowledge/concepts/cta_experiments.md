# CTA Experiments

## Definition

Systematic testing of different Call-to-Action formats at the end of LinkedIn posts to optimize for meaningful comments (weighted ~12x a like under [[360_brew]]). Q1 baseline: avg 0.5 comments per post. Target: ≥1.0 avg.

## Evidence

| Post | Date | CTA Format | Comments | Impressions | Assessment |
|:---|:---|:---|:---:|:---:|:---|
| Q1 Retro Post | 2026-03-31 | "What's one assumption data proved wrong?" | TBD | TBD | Specific + reflective — promising format |
| Manager Partnership posts (Q1) | Various | Open-ended questions | 0 avg | Variable | ❌ Open-ended CTAs fail for MP content |
| Kaizen Bridge | 2026-02-10 | Implicit (save-worthy insight) | 3 | 332 | ✅ Saves + comments without explicit CTA |
| Decision Drift | 2026-02-17 | Contrarian question | 2 | 356 | ✅ Scenario-based worked |

## Current Rule

- Explicit, scenario-based questions outperform open-ended CTAs.
- Manager Partnership posts specifically need stronger CTAs (Q1: 0 comments avg on MP content).
- Save-worthy insights at the end can substitute for explicit CTAs if the content naturally invites bookmarking.

## Evolution

- **Jan 2026:** No systematic CTA testing. Mixed results.
- **Feb 2026:** Noticed Manager Partnership content generates 0 comments regardless of CTA.
- **Mar 2026:** Identified that scenario-based questions outperform open-ended CTAs (Q1 Lessons).
- **Apr 2026:** Three-format A/B test planned (explicit question, fill-in-blank, contrast question).
- **Apr 2026 (W17):** Fill-in-the-blank CTA deployed: "The boundary I wish I had drawn earlier: ______". Also first text + AI-generated image experiment.

## Open Questions

1. Is the 0-comment problem on MP content a CTA problem or a topic problem? (MP may inherently be less comment-worthy than AI content.)
2. Does ending with a question reduce Save rate? (Saves and comments may be inversely correlated.)
3. Should we test "no CTA" as a control — just end with a mic-drop insight?
