# The Hero Bottleneck (Brent)

## Definition
From *The Phoenix Project*, "Brent" is the indispensible senior engineer who knows how to fix everything. Because he is continually pulled into Unplanned Work (Type 4) to save the day, he becomes the universal constraint. Everything routes through him, delaying planned work.

## Current Rule
- This is the archetype of the **Warrior mode** gone wrong. The Warrior jumps in to fix it (being the hero), causing the bottleneck.
- The **Statesman mode** involves protecting the bottleneck, isolating them from unplanned work so systemic flow can resume.
- Use the "Brent" archetype when discussing key-person dependencies in the Manager Partnership pillar.

## Evolution
- **Apr 2026:** Ingested to support the Warrior vs. Statesman signature framework.
- **Apr 2026 (W16):** Two drafts demonstrate the inverse — SM deliberately removes himself as a potential hero bottleneck. Both the Facilitator's Silence and PO Private Strength show that the SM IS the potential Brent when they over-insert themselves.

## Counter-Arguments & Data Gaps
1. **Against:** Sometimes teams genuinely need the SM to be "Brent" temporarily — especially during crises or when a new PO joins. Premature withdrawal could look like abandonment rather than empowerment.
2. **Data Gap:** We have no evidence of what happens when the SM steps back TOO EARLY (before Shu-Ha-Ri readiness). The Facilitator's Silence assumes the team reached Ri — but what if they hadn't?
3. **Bias Check:** Both W16 stories have positive outcomes. We should document a case where stepping back failed.
