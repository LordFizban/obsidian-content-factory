# Turkish Content Strategy

## Definition

Publishing Turkish-language content as a reach lever targeting the 67% Turkish-speaking segment of Kaan's LinkedIn audience. Turkish posts drive strong passive reach (impressions) but generate zero comments. The strategy treats this as acceptable — reach is the goal, not conversation.

## Current Rule

- **Cadence:** 1 Turkish post per month (last Thursday of the month).
- **Source:** Only translate Q1 top performers. Don't create Turkish-original content yet.
- **Translation:** Localization Lead skill applied. Preserve thought leader references, cultural nuance, and core metaphors (Lighthouse, Warrior).
- **Measurement:** Compare against W12 baseline (549 impressions, 9 reactions).
- **Expectation:** Zero comments is acceptable. Reach is enough.

## Counter-Arguments & Data Gaps

1. **Against:** "Zero comments is acceptable" may be normalizing a problem. Zero comments means zero relationship-building with Turkish-speaking professionals. If the goal is ultimately business development (not just brand awareness), then impressions without dialogue are empty reach.
2. **Against:** Translating top English performers assumes the same topics resonate with Turkish professionals. The 67% Turkish-speaking audience may have entirely different pain points (e.g., local market challenges vs. global Agile discourse).
3. **Data Gap:** n=2 Turkish posts is too small to draw any conclusions. The 549-impression result could be an outlier driven by the Kaizen topic (Lean is huge in Turkish manufacturing).
4. **Data Gap:** We have no data on Turkish-original content vs. translated content. The assumption that "translate top performers" is optimal has never been tested against Turkish-first content.
5. **Bias Check:** The "reach lever" framing may be self-serving — it lets us count Turkish content as a win without doing the harder work of building Turkish-language conversations.
