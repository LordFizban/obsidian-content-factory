# Stories vs. Frameworks

## Definition

Content grounded in a concrete, personal sprint moment (a "story") consistently outperforms content that presents abstract principles or frameworks without narrative grounding. This is the single most important content quality rule derived from Q1 2026 data.

## Evidence

| Post | Date | Type | Impressions | Eng. Rate | Category | Supports/Contradicts |
|:---|:---|:---|:---:|:---:|:---|:---|
| Respect Is Not Being Nice | 2026-03-10 | Text (story) | 653 | High | Story | ✅ Q1 ATH — grounded in a sprint moment |
| Kaizen Bridge | 2026-02-10 | Text + Image (story) | 332 | 4.5% | Story | ✅ Best engagement rate Q1 |
| Decision Drift | 2026-02-17 | Text (story) | 356 | Medium | Story | ✅ Concrete sprint velocity experiment |
| Manager's Manual | 2026-02-24 | Article (story + framework) | 357 | Low | Hybrid | 🟡 High reach, low engagement |
| Done With People | 2026-03-06 | Text (abstract) | 198 | Low | Framework | ❌ Abstract philosophy, no sprint moment |
| AI Discovery Carousel | 2026-02-05 | Carousel (framework) | 104 | Low | Framework | ❌ Framework-heavy, low narrative |

## Current Rule

**Every post needs a concrete sprint moment.** No abstract philosophy without grounding in a real event the author experienced. This rule is non-negotiable for Q2 2026.

- **Minimum standard:** At least one specific event ("Last Tuesday, during Sprint Review...") must appear in the first 40% of the post.
- **Exception:** Authority Borrowing reposts may comment on frameworks if the commentary is grounded in personal experience.

## Evolution

- **Jan 2026:** Not yet identified as a pattern. Both story and framework content published without differentiation.
- **Feb 2026:** Feb Strategy Review noted that Kaizen Bridge (story) outperformed AI Discovery Carousel (framework) by 3x on engagement rate.
- **Mar 2026:** "Respect Is Not Being Nice" (653 imps, story) vs "Done With People" (198 imps, abstract) — 3.3x reach difference. Rule crystallized.
- **Apr 2026:** Codified in April Content Plan as the #1 Q1 lesson. Applied to all April slots.

## Implications for Skills

- **Editor-in-Chief:** Consider adding a "Concrete Story" check to the Brand Voice Scoring Rubric, or making it a prerequisite for the "Authenticity" dimension (currently scores 1-5 without a story requirement).
- **Content Strategist:** Hook Generator should prioritize "Vulnerable Story" (#1) over other options when pillar alignment is equal.

## Open Questions

1. Is the story/framework distinction less important for Turkish-language content? (Turkish Kaizen Bridge performed well but was a translation of a story-driven post.)
2. Can a framework post succeed if it's wrapped in a narrative frame? (Manager's Manual was hybrid — high reach, low engagement.)
3. Does article format mitigate the framework penalty? (Articles outperform text posts on reach generally.)
