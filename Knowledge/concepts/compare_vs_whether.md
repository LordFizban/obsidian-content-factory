# Compare and Contrast vs. Whether or Not

## Definition
From Teresa Torres: A decision-making principle. Humans are susceptible to confirmation bias when facing a "whether or not" decision (e.g., "Should we build this database feature?"). We make exponentially better choices when we reframe the question to "compare and contrast" (e.g., "Which of these 3 features best solves the customer's data problem?").

- This is an operational tactic for solving the **Decision Fog** in the **Manager Partnership** pillar.
- The Agile Coach’s job is to spot "whether or not" conversations and force the team or management to generate two more alternative solutions to compare against.
