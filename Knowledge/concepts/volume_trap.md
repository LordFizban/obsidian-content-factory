# The Volume Trap

## Definition

Publishing more than 2 posts per week on LinkedIn produces *lower* total impressions than publishing exactly 2. This is a confirmed anti-pattern — volume has diminishing and then negative returns under [[360_brew]].

## Evidence

| Week | Posts | Total Impressions | Avg/Post | Notes |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---|
| W8 (Feb 17-21) | 4 | 828 | 207 | Volume experiment. 4 posts cannibalized each other. |
| W9 (Feb 24-28) | 2 | 896 | 448 | 2 posts. Higher total AND higher per-post. Q1 ATH week. |
| W6 (Feb 3-7) | 2 | 844 | 422 | Normal 2-post week. High total. |

## Current Rule

**Never exceed 2 posts/week.** Tuesday + Thursday only. No "bonus" posts.

- This is a global optimum, not a local one — confirmed across 2 independent experiments.
- The mechanism is likely 360 Brew's content lifespan (21 days). More frequent posting splits the algorithm's distribution budget for your profile.

## Evolution

- **Jan 2026:** Thursday posting validated (adds ~30% impressions over weekday average).
- **Feb 2026 (W8):** Volume experiment — 4 posts in one week. Result: 828 total imps (lower than 2-post weeks).
- **Feb 2026 (W9):** Confirmation — 2 posts, 896 total imps. Rule crystallized.
- **Mar-Apr 2026:** Rule enforced. No violations.

## Open Questions

1. Does the 2-post limit apply when one post is Turkish? (Turkish posts may target a different audience segment and not cannibalize.)
2. Would 3 posts/week work if spaced Mon-Wed-Fri instead of clustered?
3. Is the effect platform-wide or profile-specific? (Higher-follower accounts may have a larger distribution budget.)
